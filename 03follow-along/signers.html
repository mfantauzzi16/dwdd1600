Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

The Declaration of Independence, signed on July 4, 1776, had 56 signers, but some of the most famous ones are often highlighted because of their significant roles in American history or their later contributions to the nation. Here are a few of the most well-known:

Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the third president of the United States and played a key role in founding the University of Virginia.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence and a member of the committee that drafted the Declaration. Later became the second president of the U.S. and contributed to early American diplomacy.
Benjamin Franklin
A leading figure in the Continental Congress and a key negotiator in the American Revolution. Became one of the Founding Fathers and served as U.S. Ambassador to France.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress and the first person to sign the Declaration (famously with a large, bold signature). Later became governor of Massachusetts.
Samuel Adams
A key leader of the American Revolution and a founding member of the Sons of Liberty. Later served as governor of Massachusetts and continued advocating for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the resolution for independence that led to the drafting of the Declaration. Later served as a senator from Virginia and an influential voice in the early Republic.
James Madison
Although he didn't sign the Declaration, Madison played a key role in drafting the U.S. Constitution. Later became the fourth president of the United States.
Thomas Paine
Although not a signer, Paine's pamphlet *Common Sense* heavily influenced public opinion in favor of independence. He continued writing influential works, such as *The American Crisis*, during the Revolution.

These individuals are some of the most famous names, but the document itself is a product of collective effort from many statesmen. The Declaration of Independence itself, and those who signed it, played a pivotal role in establishing the United States as an independent nation.
